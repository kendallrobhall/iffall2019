Homemade

Cinnamon Toast Crunch

Description

Words cannot describe how much better this is than that old boxed stuff. These are light, buttery, crunchy and cinnamony.

Information

  • prep time: 20 minutes
  • cook time: 13 minutes
  • total time: 33 minutes
  • servings: 6
  • calories: 435 kcal

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups whole wheat pastry flour
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1/3 cup + 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/8 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla
  • 2 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1/8 cup sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Line two baking sheets with a silpat or parchment paper.
  2. In the bowl of a food processor add the whole wheat flour, flour, baking soda, and 2 teaspoons cinnamon.
  3. Pulse to combine. Then add in 1/3 cup coconut oil, 1/3 cup brown sugar, 1/8 cup sugar, vanilla, and honey.
  4. Process until the dough looks like small peas, then add in the buttermilk and process until a dough ball forms.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees
  6. Dump the dough out onto a lightly floured counter and pinch any crumbles of dough together with your hands. Divide the dough into two flat disks.
  7. Working with one piece of dough at a time place a piece of wax paper over the dough and roll the dough as thin as you can, no thicker than an inch thick, but preferably a 1/8 inch thick (about the thickness of a quarter).
  8. Remove the top piece of parchment paper.
  9. In a small bowl combine the 1/8 cup sugar and 3/4 teaspoon cinnamon. In another bowl melt the remaining 1 tablespoon coconut oil in the microwave.
  10. Brush the whole sheet of dough with the melted coconut oil and then generously sprinkle with the cinnamon sugar.
  11. With a pastry cutter, pizza cutter or very sharp knife, even the edges of the rectangle. Slice into pieces, about 1/2 inch squares.
  12. Pierce each square carefully 2 or 3 with a fork.
  13. Use a very thin spatula and scrape the squares off the counter and then carefully use your hands to place the squares on the prepared baking sheets.
  14. Repeat with the remaining dough until all the dough has been used. Bake for 9 to 13 minutes or until lightly golden brown on top.
  15. Allow to cool completely and store in an airtight container.
